  • One and the same company is a

    “creditor” (payee) in others.
  • Each commitment in the system is to be confirmed by both the creditor and the debtor. The fact of registration of the commitment in the system implies that the debtor agrees with the debt amount and guarantees its payment upon first request of the creditor.
  • You can register a commitment not for the full amount of the deal, but only for its part (and pay the rest as usual).

On the one hand, we have an interest-free “installment plan” as the debtor for an indefinite period. On the other hand, you as a “creditor” are not a “victim” and do not credit anyone, because you fully use commitments to you to settle accounts with your own creditors. You need to make payments in favor of other companies, don’t you? This saves your money on bank fees, even if your creditors are in other jurisdictions.

...and then

  • The system constantly monitors companies with favorable balances of incoming commitments (accounts receivable) and outgoing commitments (accounts payable). When it detects such a combination, Combank tries to offset them with one another.
  • It works by generating and concluding trilateral agreements with two operations on behalf of Combank clients. First, the old creditor transfers the debt of one of the debtors in favor of a new creditor. Second, another commitment (from the old creditor to the new creditor) is offset. This is because the assignment of the right to claim the debt needed to be paid for at the first stage.
